# The Garden — Landscape Crawler Instructions

## What we're building

A map of organisations, companies, networks, research groups, movements, and initiatives worldwide that are — knowingly or not — already doing work that fits within the vision of The Garden: a project to prototype planetary governance through play, simulation, and embodied experience.

The map is organised around four orientations:

**Garden of Eden** — Living systems, ecology, land stewardship, regenerative economics, indigenous knowledge, biodiversity, food systems, water, soil. The orientation of tending and cultivation.

**Spaceship Earth** — Technology, infrastructure, data, digital twins, AI, blockchain, spatial computing, governance tools, transparency systems, alternative ownership models. The orientation of building and systems design.

**Eleusinian Mysteries** — Transformative games, LARP, ritual, ceremony, experiential futures, festival culture, consciousness research, performance, art for social change, the psychology of awe. The orientation of meaning, transformation, and embodied experience.

**General Assembly of Earth** — Global governance innovation, participatory democracy, citizens' assemblies, rights of nature, climate justice, long-termism, planetary health, education for planetary citizenship. The cross-cutting meta-frame where the other three meet.

## How to crawl

### Step 1: Use the seed actors as starting points

The spreadsheet (Sheet 2: Seed Actors) contains ~36 known organisations. Use these as entry points. For each seed actor, find:
- Their partners and collaborators (listed on their websites, in their reports, in press releases)
- Organisations they link to, cite, or co-publish with
- People on their advisory boards or in their leadership who also work at other relevant orgs
- Events they attend or sponsor (conference attendee lists are rich hunting grounds)

### Step 2: Use the search terms to find new actors

Sheet 1 contains 30 categories, each with 4-6 specific search terms. Run these against:
- Google / Bing (web results)
- Google Scholar (academic actors)
- LinkedIn (companies and people)
- Crunchbase / AngelList (startups)
- GitHub (open source projects)
- Twitter/X and Bluesky (movements and networks)

### Step 3: For each actor found, capture these fields

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Name | Organisation / company / network / person name |
| Type | NGO, Company, Research, Government, Network, Movement, Person |
| Primary orientation | Garden of Eden / Spaceship Earth / Eleusinian Mysteries / General Assembly of Earth |
| Secondary orientation | If applicable — many actors span two |
| Category | Which of the 30 categories (from Sheet 1) they best fit |
| Description | 1-2 sentences on what they do |
| Location | Country or region |
| URL | Main website |
| Scale | Local / National / Regional / Global |
| Maturity | Idea / Early / Active / Established |
| Relevance score | 1-5 (how directly relevant to The Garden's vision) |
| Connection | How we found them (which seed actor, which search term) |
| Contact pathway | Do we know someone who knows them? |

### Step 4: Prioritise quality over quantity

We don't need 10,000 entries. We need 200-500 well-mapped actors that are genuinely doing relevant work. Filter aggressively:

**Include if:**
- They are actively doing something (not just talking about it)
- Their work directly connects to at least one of the four orientations
- They have real outputs: projects, publications, events, products, communities
- They could plausibly participate in, benefit from, or strengthen a governance LARP / simulation / assembly

**Exclude if:**
- They are purely commercial with no governance / ecological / social dimension
- They are defunct or inactive (no updates in 2+ years)
- They are a single blog post or thought piece, not an organisation or initiative
- They are primarily focused on AI safety / alignment debates without governance application

### Step 5: Flag high-value connections

Mark any actor where:
- They are already connected to someone in our network (Sheet 3: People Map)
- They are doing something so close to The Garden's vision that they might want to collaborate directly
- They are running events where The Garden could be presented or prototyped
- They offer funding or grants relevant to our work
- They are based in Sweden or the Nordics (easier to reach for first prototype)

## Orientation classification guide

Some actors will be hard to classify. Use this logic:

**If they primarily work with land, ecosystems, food, water, biodiversity, or indigenous knowledge → Garden of Eden**

**If they primarily build technology, platforms, data systems, governance tools, economic infrastructure, or alternative ownership models → Spaceship Earth**

**If they primarily create experiences, games, rituals, art, performance, festivals, or work with transformation, consciousness, or meaning-making → Eleusinian Mysteries**

**If they primarily work on global governance structures, democracy innovation, rights frameworks, climate justice, or planetary-scale institutional design → General Assembly of Earth**

**If they do two of these equally → list both, primary first**

## Notes

- This map is a living document. It will grow and change as the project develops.
- The four orientations are not rigid categories — they are lenses. Many of the most interesting actors will sit at the boundaries between two or three.
- The map serves two purposes: (1) understanding the landscape of who is already working on pieces of this vision, and (2) identifying potential collaborators, participants, and allies for The Garden's first prototype and beyond.
- When in doubt about whether to include something, include it. We can filter later. We can't find what we didn't look for.
