# Research Brief: The Garden Landscape — Actors in Planetary Governance Prototyping

This comprehensive research report maps global organisations, networks, and initiatives developing infrastructure for planetary governance, simulation, and ecological stewardship. Key findings indicate that a robust, decentralised ecosystem of actors is actively building tools that bridge physical ecology and digital governance. Research suggests that the intersection of regenerative finance (ReFi), spatial computing, and decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs) forms the core of emerging "Spaceship Earth" and "Garden of Eden" initiatives. It seems likely that the integration of artificial intelligence with earth observation data is accelerating the creation of high-fidelity planetary digital twins. The evidence leans toward an increasing collaboration between legacy institutions and Web3 innovators to address systemic climate and biodiversity challenges. This report provides an exhaustive deep-dive into fifteen high-priority actors, detailing their operations, governance models, and potential as collaborators for The Garden's planetary governance prototyping.

## Conceptual Framework: Planetary Governance Infrastructure

The objective of this research is to evaluate high-priority actors across four specific orientations. The "Garden of Eden" orientation encompasses living systems, ecology, land stewardship, and regenerative economics. The "Spaceship Earth" orientation focuses on technology, infrastructure, governance tools, and alternative ownership models. The "Eleusinian Mysteries" orientation involves transformative games, live-action role-playing (LARP), ritual, and experiential futures. The "General Assembly of Earth" orientation targets global governance innovation, participatory democracy, and the rights of nature. The actors analysed in this report predominantly occupy the Garden of Eden and Spaceship Earth orientations, providing the critical physical and digital infrastructure required to prototype planetary governance systems.

### What They Do
Planetary Guardians is a regenerative governance protocol designed for landscape-scale ecological stewardship, while SpiralWeb serves as its coordination infrastructure and knowledge commons [cite: 13]. The initiative connects place-based land stewards through a formalised protocol architecture [cite: 15]. SpiralWeb operates through "Field Nodes," grounding theoretical governance in highly specific, localized interventions [cite: 13, 15]. 

The system operates on an evidence-bound logic, where the base unit of stewardship is a 10-square-metre "pixel" of land. Scaling occurs only when ecological indicators confirm stability and steward capacity is verified [cite: 13]. The architecture includes four layers: narrative orientation (cafés), field nodes, the protocol stack (methods and agreements), and a developing machine layer for evidence ledgers [cite: 13, 15]. The founder, Lars A. Engberg, brings decades of experience in meta-governance, urban climate adaptation, and participatory design from Danish and Swedish contexts [cite: 12, 14, 16].

### Why They Matter for Planetary Governance Prototyping
SpiralWeb presents a highly rigorous, Ostrom-inspired protocol for connecting micro-level ecological actions (10 m² pixels) to macro-level planetary governance [cite: 13]. Their distinction between different support architectures (Land, Cultural, and Coordination) provides a readymade framework for a regenerative economy game mechanic [cite: 15]. The focus on "meta-governance"—steering relations and negotiations between actors without top-down coercion—is highly applicable to designing General Assembly simulators [cite: 12, 14]. Their use of JSON schemas and evidence ledgers bridges the Garden of Eden ecological focus with Spaceship Earth technological verifiability [cite: 13].

### What They Do

The coalition tracks emissions from 745 million individual emitting assets—such as power plants, steel mills, ships, and farms—and aggregates this data into 2.7 million public sources [cite: 32, 36]. Led by former U.S. Vice President Al Gore and coordinated by WattTime, the coalition aims to facilitate radical transparency, enabling governments, companies, and activists to verify pledges to cut emissions and hold large emitters accountable [cite: 33, 34, 35].

Beyond enforcement, RFCx uses advanced Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) developed alongside Microsoft AI for Good to identify hundreds of animal species from audio recordings [cite: 37]. Their Arbimon platform allows local scientists and ecologists to conduct long-term biodiversity monitoring, measuring the success of wildlife restoration efforts and uncovering new species [cite: 37, 40, 41].
