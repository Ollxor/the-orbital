# Research Brief: Garden Landscape — Deep-Dive

This report presents a comprehensive mapping of fifteen high-priority actors relevant to **The Garden**, an initiative aimed at prototyping planetary governance through play, simulation, and embodied experience. The synthesized intelligence is structured across four specific orientations: *Garden of Eden* (ecology and land stewardship), *Spaceship Earth* (technology and governance tools), *Eleusinian Mysteries* (transformative games and LARPs), and *General Assembly of Earth* (global governance innovation and participatory democracy). 

The evidence points to an emerging, highly interconnected ecosystem of organizations working on fragmented but complementary aspects of planetary resilience. While some actors are deeply embedded in technical infrastructure (e.g., OpenEarth Foundation, Regen Network) or policy innovation (e.g., Sortition Foundation, Future Generations Commissioner for Wales), others are directly bridging the gap between experiential futures and governance (e.g., Metagov, Not Only Larp, World Game Workshop). Several Nordic-based entities demonstrate strong institutional backing for global governance reform, notably the Global Challenges Foundation and Naturskyddsföreningen. It seems highly likely that synthesizing the outputs of these specific actors—from open-source deliberation toolkits to spatial ecological data and immersive role-play frameworks—can provide the exact scaffolding required for a planetary governance simulation. The following sections provide an exhaustive, fact-dense extraction of each target actor based on the available research intelligence.

## Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature (GARN)

**Name:** Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature (GARN)
**Type:** International Network / NGO
**Orientation:** GARDEN
**Website:** https://www.garn.org
**Location:** Global
**Scale:** Large (International)
**Maturity:** Mature (Founded 2010)
**Flags:** Direct Collaborator Potential (High)

**What they do:**
GARN is an international network dedicated to advancing the legal recognition that nature is a fundamental, rights-bearing entity [cite: 1, 2]. Founded in 2010 following the World People's Conference on Climate Change in Bolivia, GARN aims to address the polycrisis of climate change, biodiversity loss, and social inequality by replacing exploitative environmental laws with systems that honor the interconnectedness of all life [cite: 1]. The organization serves as a global hub connecting the Rights of Nature movement through campaigns, knowledge exchange, and coalition building [cite: 1]. A primary activity is the orchestration of the International Rights of Nature Tribunal, an international governing institution established in 2014 by citizens to investigate human rights violations and foster international Rights of Nature law [cite: 3].
