# Research Brief: Garden Landscape — Nordic Cluster Expansion

Research suggests that the Nordic region possesses a highly developed, albeit fragmented, ecosystem of organizations dedicated to democratic innovation, ecological regeneration, and civic technology. It seems likely that integrating these disparate actors could significantly advance the prototyping of planetary governance. The evidence leans toward a strong existing foundation in institutionalized deliberative democracy (especially in Finland and Denmark) and emerging rights of nature advocacy (prominently in Sweden), which can directly supplement The Garden's identified coverage gaps.

The following brief synthesizes a comprehensive landscape scan of the Nordic region, targeting the specific strategic orientations of the General Assembly of Earth (**ASSEMBLY**), the Garden of Eden (**GARDEN**), and Spaceship Earth (**SPACESHIP**). The region exhibits a unique sociopolitical climate characterized by high institutional trust, making it a fertile testing ground for new governance models. However, mapping these actors reveals inherent complexities; many organizations operate at the intersection of technology and ecology, or governance and land stewardship, blurring the boundaries between designated orientations. 

To systematically evaluate the potential integration of these entities into The Garden's framework, a relevance heuristic can be applied. If we define relevance \( R \) as a function of an actor's alignment with planetary governance (\( G \)), their utilization of scalable or experiential methodologies (\( M \)), and their network connectivity (\( C \)), we might conceptualize this relationship as \[ R = \alpha G + \beta M + \gamma C \] where the coefficients denote the strategic weighting for The Garden's immediate prototyping needs. The profiles detailed below highlight actors who score highly across these dimensions, offering critical pathways for collaboration.

Their engagement with the Sámi provides a tangible bridge between ecological restoration and indigenous rights.
