The V-Dem Institute at the University of Gothenburg has released its annual democracy assessment covering 2025, and the picture is stark: democratic gains from the third wave of democratization since the mid-1970s are "almost eradicated." For the average person on Earth, democracy stands at 1978 levels. The institute now counts 92 autocracies against 87 democracies among 179 countries assessed, with 6 billion people — 74 percent of humanity — living under autocratic rule. Just 600 million live in liberal democracies.

The report identifies 44 countries currently autocratizing, home to 41 percent of the global population, while only 18 countries representing 5 percent of humanity are democratizing. Among 2025's new autocratizers: the United States, which V-Dem says has fallen from liberal democracy to electoral democracy for the first time in over 50 years, with democratic quality now at 1965 levels. The institute describes the speed of decline under Trump's second presidency as "unprecedented in modern history," with legislative constraints reaching their lowest point in over a century.

Freedom of expression worsened in 44 countries during 2025, while torture to suppress opposition increased in 33. Four of the world's five most populous nations — India, China, Indonesia, and Pakistan — are autocracies. The report notes more people now live in closed autocracies than in electoral and liberal democracies combined.

This is governance measured at scale, not debated in theory — a dataset tracking how power actually distributes and constrains itself across 179 polities. The trends suggest that electoral and representative systems, absent deeper structural transformation, may be insufficient to resist authoritarian drift. What V-Dem tracks is not merely democratic "backsliding" but a question of whether existing governance forms can adapt to planetary pressures — or whether they calcify into control.
