The Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature is opening a new round of internships across four areas: communications, design and web development, the International Rights of Nature Tribunal, and organizing. The positions, lasting two to six months, can be completed remotely or in-person at GARN's Quito headquarters — though in-person interns must cover their own expenses.

GARN operates as a coordinating node for the Rights of Nature movement, which seeks legal recognition that ecosystems have intrinsic rights to exist, flourish, and regenerate. That work includes supporting Indigenous communities, advancing test cases, and hosting tribunals that function as both legal proceedings and public rituals of accountability.

The internships offer a window into how social movements build institutional capacity — not through polished advocacy alone, but through the unglamorous work of documentation, web infrastructure, event coordination, and multilingual storytelling. Applications close January 20, and no prior Rights of Nature experience is required.

It's a model worth noting: movements that aim to shift legal paradigms need people who can build websites, draft tribunal summaries, and manage cross-regional partnerships. Governance innovation depends on this kind of distributed, often volunteer labor — a reality that raises questions about sustainability even as it demonstrates commitment.
