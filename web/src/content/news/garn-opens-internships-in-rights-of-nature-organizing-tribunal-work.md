The Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature is opening a new round of two-to-six-month internships across four areas: communications, design and web development, support for the International Rights of Nature Tribunal, and organizing. The positions are remote and unpaid, though in-person placements in Quito, Ecuador are available for those who can self-fund.

Based in Quito, GARN functions as a coordinating node for the Rights of Nature movement — the legal and political project to recognize ecosystems as rights-bearing entities rather than property. The organization supports Indigenous communities, advances legal strategies, and convenes the tribunal, which operates as both adjudicative body and public pedagogy, hearing cases on deep-sea mining, algorithmic water management, and deforestation.

The internships offer a rare entry point into governance experimentation that is already operational — not speculative. GARN is explicit that prior expertise in Rights of Nature is not required; commitment and collaborative capacity matter more. Applications close January 20, and details are available at garn.org/internships.

This is less about résumé-building than about joining infrastructure that is already rewriting the legal relationship between human systems and living worlds — one case, one jurisdiction, one organizing cycle at a time.
