The Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature is recruiting interns across four domains: communications, design and web development, tribunal support, and organizing. The positions are remote and run two to six months, with applications closing January 20.

The tribunal role stands out — interns will assist with research, documentation, and coordination for sessions of the International Rights of Nature Tribunal, which operates as both legal proceeding and public ritual. GARN, based in Quito, coordinates the global network advancing Rights of Nature frameworks: legal structures that grant ecosystems standing to exist, flourish, and regenerate.

This is governance work in practice, not in theory. Interns won't observe from the sidelines — they'll contribute to cases, campaigns, and coordination across a movement testing how societies embed ecological personhood into law. The model matters: networked, values-driven, and learning by doing.

No prior Rights of Nature experience required. GARN emphasizes commitment over credentials, recognizing that transformative governance needs multiple disciplines — law, yes, but also storytelling, design, organizing, and the willingness to work across borders and epistemologies.
