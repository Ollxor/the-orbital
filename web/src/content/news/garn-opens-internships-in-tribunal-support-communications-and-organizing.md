The Global Alliance for the Rights of Nature has opened applications for a new cohort of interns to support the infrastructure of the Rights of Nature movement — including research and coordination for its International Rights of Nature Tribunal, communications work, web development, and organizing across regions.

The positions, running two to six months and available remotely or in-person in Quito, Ecuador, represent the practical scaffolding required to operate what GARN describes as a "central node" of global legal and activist networks. The tribunal component is particularly notable: applicants can assist with research, documentation, and communications related to sessions that function as governance performance — testing legal theories of ecological personhood in settings that blend jurisprudence, ritual, and public pedagogy.

GARN's framing is instructive. The organization positions the work not as conventional advocacy but as "advancing the Rights of Nature in practice" — a distinction that reflects the movement's understanding that new governance logics require new institutions, not just new arguments. Interns covering their own expenses in Quito will work alongside what the organization calls a "values-driven team" spanning law, media, academia, and community organizing.

Applications close January 20. No prior Rights of Nature experience required — though the work itself, coordinating tribunals and legal strategies across continents, offers a view into how governance innovations move from concept to courtroom to campaign.
