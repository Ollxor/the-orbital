Polling from Democracy Without Borders' 2025 Global Solidarity Report reveals an uncomfortable pattern: support for international cooperation is declining across countries, generations, and income levels. Among 22,000 respondents across 31 countries, the proportion who identify as global citizens, support taxes for global problems, or want enforcement powers for international institutions all dropped from 2024 to 2025. Even younger generations — often assumed to lean internationalist — now match their grandparents' skepticism.

The diagnosis points to a governance problem, not a values problem. As the report notes, domestic institutions like public hospitals remain politically untouchable, while withdrawals from the World Health Organization are treated as legitimate policy choices. The difference isn't philosophical — it's whether people believe institutions deliver. "International cooperation has not yet been anchored in public expectations in the same way domestic institutions are," the authors write. That anchoring requires visible results, not rhetoric.

The report highlights wealth inequality as a test case for whether cooperation can restore faith in collective problem-solving. Financial capital crosses borders as fluidly as carbon emissions or viruses, yet governance systems remain fragmented. A UN tax convention is now formally underway — a world-first effort that, for all its political obstacles, demonstrates that multilateral solutions are already in motion. Brazil's G20 leadership secured a landmark declaration on international tax cooperation in 2025, and even in the U.S., where the current administration opposes the convention, New York's mayoral election saw a pro-tax-the-rich candidate win.

The underlying thesis applies to all scales of governance: institutions succeed when people feel ownership and see results. Global cooperation isn't optional for planetary challenges — pandemics, climate breakdown, conflict, and inequality don't respect borders. But legitimacy can't be assumed. It has to be built through performance that people can feel.
